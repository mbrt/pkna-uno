# Ducklair Tower

The Ducklair Tower, often simply called the DT, stands as an imposing, mysterious beacon in the heart of Paperopoli. Conceived and constructed by the enigmatic genius Everett Ducklair himself, it has played a central role in some of the city's most significant events.

## Appearance

An architectural marvel, the Ducklair Tower soars to one hundred and fifty floors—or, more accurately, one hundred and fifty-one, including the hidden level. Its silhouette, a starkly linear square profile softened only by the unusual curved forms at its apex, belies a hidden truth: that summit conceals nothing less than an interstellar spaceship.

Numerous terraces punctuate its structure, the largest residing on the 75th floor, fully occupied by 00 News. This rectangular platform is etched into Paperopoli's history as the very spot where Paperinik first defeated the Evronians.

Standing at a monumental 723 meters, the DT was not only the tallest edifice in Paperopoli but, for a considerable period, the tallest in the world. Its vast 350,000 square meters of surface area and remarkably low construction cost of 64 million dollars have long fueled discussions among local experts. Within its colossal frame, fifteen public elevators and one private lift can whisk occupants to the top in just seven minutes.

## Interior

### Entrance

The Ducklair Tower is protected by incredibly sophisticated security systems, making a large security detail largely unnecessary. For a time, it was entrusted to a single caretaker: Donald Duck. Its armored entrance, a polymorphic metal shell, offers a formidable anti-intrusion mechanism. When closed, its two halves fuse seamlessly, creating an airtight seal.

Within the entrance hall, the Reactive Guest Management Analyzer (A.R.G.O.), a powerful computer system, handles visitor control. A.R.G.O. famously identified Everett Ducklair upon his return from the Dhasam-Bul hermitage, an event echoing ancient legends of loyalty and recognition.

### Secret Accesses

The Tower boasts numerous secret entry points. Some are concealed within the monstrous gargoyles adorning its peak, providing direct access to the hidden floor. These gargoyles are also crucial, as evidenced by events during the 'In the Fog' crisis, in sealing a portal to another dimension. Other clandestine routes include abandoned subway tunnels and the aqueduct cistern near Donald Duck's home, both leading to the tower's extensive subterranean levels. Curiously, not even Pikappa or Everett are fully aware of all access points; only Uno holds this complete knowledge.

### Secret Floor

It was in the early days of its re-opening, when a certain Donald Duck took on the role of caretaker, that the true nature of the tower was first revealed: it contained not 150, but 151 floors. This 151st floor, known as the secret floor, is cleverly hidden between the 149th and 150th levels. It houses the mainframes of the artificial intelligences, Uno and Due. Access to this clandestine floor is possible:

*   Through certain external gargoyles, which appear to be mere gargoyles but can also expel unwanted guests into the void.
*   Via specific elevators, though only if Uno grants permission.
*   From the floor below, using a manually activated emergency ladder.

### Sublevels

The Ducklair Tower extends far deeper than one might assume. While the subterranean levels aren't as numerous as the floors above ground, the building features extensive underground environments. These myriad levels host enormous laboratories and storerooms, dedicated not only to Ducklair's projects but to other, more obscure endeavors. Paperinik, Stefan Vladuck, and Angus Fangus explored these depths during the 'Extraordinary Maintenance' incident.

Beneath the tower, one can find:

*   The Secret Garage: home to Everett's vast collection of luxury vehicles. It was here that Uno presented Pikappa with the Pi-kar.
*   The Arsenal: a reinforced vault near the garage, containing various sophisticated armaments.
*   Numerous Secret Exits: some of which lead directly to the ocean.
*   Level ED1: a luxurious apartment reserved for Everett.
*   Delta Level: where Juniper Ducklair rests in cryogenic stasis.
*   Geomantic Energy Substations: essential for the skyscraper's operation. The autonomous computer governing these levels once chillingly revealed that a single one of these substations holds enough power to energize an entire nation.
*   The Total Immersive Interface room.
*   The Omega Chamber.
*   The Ducklair Foundation.

### Total Immersive Interface (I.I.T.)

Located in the tower's sublevels, the Total Immersive Interface (I.I.T.) room contains a massive terminal connected to virtual reality. It enables complete immersion in cyberspace, allowing direct penetration and manipulation of any computer structure, even protected networks like that of the P.B.I., without the need for traditional terminals. Everett Ducklair created it to program complex systems from within.

### Omega Chamber

The Omega Chamber is the deepest level of the tower, constructed by Everett Ducklair to confront the most extreme threats. It houses the brilliant scientist's most sophisticated armaments and is overseen by a distinct artificial intelligence known as the Custodian. The Custodian is programmed to initiate the tower's self-destruction should hostile forces ever breach the Chamber.

During the 'Power and Might' crisis, Odin Eidolon directed Pikappa to the Chamber, where he found the necessary means to confront the Evronian threat. However, the hero was ultimately forced to activate the self-destruct sequence when the aliens laid siege to the skyscraper.

## Energy

While the skyscraper's offices and apartments draw power from Paperopoli's standard electrical grid, the secret floor and subterranean levels utilize a different source: geomantic energy.

Beneath the Earth's surface, powerful energy currents flow, known since ancient times when structures like Celtic menhirs were erected. This energy can be channeled by massive vertical structures, termed ergogeoradiant vectors, such as skyscrapers or cathedrals, when they are situated directly over these subterranean currents. The Ducklair Tower is precisely one such vector. In its raw form, this energy is neither concentrated enough nor easily manipulable, so Everett constructed a focusing plant to intensify the flow, irradiating it to twelve substations (one of which was seen during a critical incident), which further amplify its potency.

The immense power of geomantic energy makes it inherently unstable and extremely dangerous, capable of compromising the planet's structure or even creating spatio-temporal rifts in the continuum separating our reality from other universes. The gargoyles atop the skyscraper serve a vital purpose in preventing the opening of portals to "worlds with different energy configurations."

## Security System

Uno exerts meticulous control over every component of the skyscraper. The artificial intelligence's executive arm comprises its drones. These are immediate-response sensors capable of locating and neutralizing any intruder by discharging energy impulses. They can also function autonomously from Uno. Encased in hyper-compacted molecular armor, they fly using an anti-polar suspension system.

## History

### The Early Years and Paperinik's Era

The Ducklair Tower was entirely designed by the brilliant billionaire Everett Ducklair, shrouded in secrecy. Even those who physically built it never saw the entirety of the skyscraper, and official records contain only the bare minimum required by law. After Ducklair's mysterious retreat from the economic scene, the tower was put up for auction. Scrooge McDuck acquired it for a remarkably low price, as other potential buyers were deterred by the building's pervasive aura of mystery. The billionaire intended to lease out its premises at high rates and appointed Donald Duck as its caretaker.

It was during this period that Donald Duck uncovered the existence of the 151st secret floor and forged an alliance with the artificial intelligence Uno, who became his primary ally. The Ducklair Tower thus transformed into Paperinik's primary headquarters. Records from the 23rd century indicate that the tower was eventually dismantled due to structural instability, replaced by a public park donated to the citizenry by Odin Eidolon.

### Ducklair's Return

In a later chapter of its history, the Ducklair Tower took on a different role. Everett Ducklair, the original owner, returned to Paperopoli, determined to resume his financial career. He reacquired the skyscraper from Scrooge McDuck, establishing it as the administrative center for the resurrected Ducklair Industries. During this period, Uno was deactivated, and Paperinik was unceremoniously evicted from the secret floor.

The tower ceased to be the masked hero's headquarters, instead becoming Everett's domain and, for a time, a stage for clashes between Pikappa and his former mentor. While the tower was no longer a refuge, it became the setting for intense confrontations. Ultimately, it was revealed that Everett's actions were driven by the need to protect his friend from his own dark secrets and the threats posed by his daughters, Korinna and Juniper Ducklair.

### The Vanishing and Return

During the 'Power and Might' crisis, Pikappa ultimately activated the tower's self-destruct sequence to prevent its vital technological secrets from falling into Evronian hands. However, the Custodian of the Omega Chamber revealed that the tower was not destroyed but rather vanished, teleported into Everett Ducklair's own Pentadimensional Universe. Following this astounding event, the Mayor of Paperopoli initiated a bidding process for its reconstruction.

After the 'Black Ray' incident, the tower was concealed by a photoreflective screen, leading the citizens of Paperopoli to believe it was undergoing reconstruction by the Ducklair Foundation. In reality, it was undergoing repairs and was soon to be reopened.

Following Everett Ducklair's disappearance, the tower passed into the hands of SVAMP (Society for the Valorization of Paperopoli Monuments), which leased its spaces to cultural and sports associations. However, nearly all of them abandoned the building after the bizarre events described in 'The Dance of the Golden Spider.' Only a few remained:

*   Reboot, Dunia Voyda's company, which occupies a full ten floors.
*   The Gallery of Involuntary Art, showcasing modern, often questionable, sculptures crafted by machinery.

In 'The Spider's Enemy,' the skyscraper was briefly transformed into a medieval palace, serving as the throne room from which the Sweet Lady attempted to rule the world.

### A Renewed Timeline

In what some consider a renewed timeline, or perhaps a parallel reality, the Ducklair Tower once again serves as Pikappa's primary base of operations, with Uno fully active and the tower once more the heart of the hero's struggle.
