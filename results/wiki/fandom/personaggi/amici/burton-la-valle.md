# Burton La Valle

Burton La Valle is a celebrated figure from 23rd-century Duckburg, renowned for his work as an actor on the pivù, the pervasive communication medium of the future. He has forged a strong professional alliance with the esteemed director and producer Ducklas Styvesant.

His most prominent involvement came during the acclaimed historical docudrama *Portrait of the Hero as a Young Duck*, and later in the popular comedic series *The Great Burton La Valle*.

## Aspetto fisico

Burton La Valle is an anthropomorphic duck, noted for his athletic and muscular physique, considerable height, and long beak. In keeping with 23rd-century aesthetic sensibilities, he maintains long blond hair, extending to his lower back, typically styled in a ponytail.

## Carattere

Burton is widely regarded as a superficial and narcissistic individual, singularly focused on his public image. As a premier celebrity of his era, he revels in opulence and being the constant center of media attention. While his artistic partnership with director Ducklas Styvesant has proven highly profitable, Burton consistently strives to impose his preferences regarding costumes and script, ensuring his image is perpetually glorified.

## Storia

Burton La Valle first rose to significant public awareness during the production of the historical re-enactment series, *Portrait of the Hero as a Young Duck*. During this groundbreaking production, the visionary director Ducklas Styvesant orchestrated the transport of Pikappa from the past to the year 2255 to record episodes for a television series centered on the ancient hero of Duckburg. Styvesant, in his relentless pursuit of realism, maintained an elaborate deception, convincing Pikappa that he was the Minister of Space-Time in dire need of assistance against a resurgent Evronian threat. In this grand illusion, Burton La Valle portrayed the valiant leader commanding the terrestrial forces.

It was only through the keen insight of Odin Eidolon that Pikappa ultimately uncovered the sophisticated deception. Following Styvesant's earnest explanations, Pikappa, demonstrating remarkable composure, magnanimously agreed to complete the final scenes of the series.

During these climactic recordings, a grave incident transpired: Styvesant's assistant, Grrodon, shockingly revealed himself to be the last surviving Evronian on Earth. He attempted to eliminate the hero, presumably to avert the eventual downfall of his species.

Following the immense success and critical acclaim of the Pikappa series, Burton La Valle continued to dominate the pivù, consistently delivering hit productions in collaboration with his preferred director, Ducklas Styvesant.
