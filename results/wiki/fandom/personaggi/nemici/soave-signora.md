# Soave Signora

The Gentle Lady, known in her youth as Miu Tay, emerged as a formidable adversary to Pikappa during the tumultuous period of the Golden Spider. First encountered during the events of "The Dance of the Golden Spider," she leads the enigmatic Golden Spider sect, a collective dedicated to forging a world of absolute perfection. Her profound danger lies in her unique mastery of both ancient mystical arts and advanced scientific principles.

## Character and Physical Appearance

The Gentle Lady's paramount ability is the manipulation of consciousness, enabling her to transplant minds into a parallel dimension—a realm entirely born of imagination, yet indistinguishable from reality itself. Furthermore, through sheer mental force, she commands machinery and manifests formidable automatons known as the Amorfi, animating inert matter to serve her will. Her formidable power is magnified by intricate devices, which she operates through precise, ritualistic dances, guided by a personal code inscrutable to all others. This unparalleled fusion of mystic prowess and technological insight allowed her, albeit temporarily, to seize control as the world's sovereign. However, even her formidable powers possess limitations: they prove ineffective against alien intelligences, such as those of Xadhoom or Troglo, or against extraterrestrial technology.

Among Pikappa's numerous adversaries, the Gentle Lady stands as a figure of profound ambiguity and contradiction, perhaps even more so than Everett Ducklair himself. She is not driven by malice; she genuinely believes in her philanthropic vision. Yet, her proposed perfect world of enforced peace and unwavering labor would extinguish free will, ushering in an era of universal servitude. She shies from violence, preferring to convert her adversaries to her cause rather than eliminate them. Nonetheless, she is unyielding and resolute in her pursuits, showing no hesitation in employing deception and manipulation. Towards her disciples, she displays a benevolent facade, earning their absolute devotion; however, she exacts a merciless price from those who disappoint her, as demonstrated by the unfortunate fate of Bulbi.

Physically, the Gentle Lady possesses an arresting grace: tall and slender, with striking blue eyes and long blonde hair intricately braided, cascading over her shoulders. Her attire is a simple green tunic, adorned only by two prominent earrings.

## History

The true origins of the Gentle Lady, Miu Tay, are meticulously documented by the Prior of Dhasam-Bui in his chronicles, "Reckoning with Reality." She is the daughter of Miu Khan, chieftain of the Chootan marauders, who once terrorized the lands of Tibet. From a tender age, Miu Tay developed her pacifist ideals as a profound reaction against her father's inherent violence and greed. When the Tibetan authorities finally dismantled Miu Khan's notorious band, the brigand, before his own disappearance, entrusted his daughter to the monastic order of Dhasam-Bul.

Within the sacred walls of the monastery, Miu Tay swiftly demonstrated mental abilities far surpassing even those of her venerable masters. It was here, too, that she forged a significant bond with Everett Ducklair, who unveiled to her an equally captivating realm: the wonders of science. Thus, following Everett's departure, the young woman, with the full consent of the monks, also left the convent to immerse herself in modern knowledge and culture.

By the time of her documented emergence in "The Dance of the Golden Spider," Miu Tay had already succumbed to the seductive influence of ambition. Under her new guise as the Gentle Lady, she commanded the Golden Spider Sect from a clandestine stronghold located in the uncharted reaches of the Far East. Her grand design for global dominion necessitated the Ducklair Tower, a structure she intended to harness as an energy catalyst. This ambition inevitably set her on a collision course with Pikappa. Initially, the Gentle Lady operated through her loyal adherent, the bodybuilder Bulbi, who secured a floor in the skyscraper under the guise of an aerobics studio. For a time, Bulbi succeeded in seizing the minds of nearly all Paperopoli's citizens, transferring them into the Gentle Lady's utopian, illusory world. However, Pikappa, alongside Uno and Dunia Voyda, combined their unique strengths to restore reality, while Xadhoom, her alien mind impervious to the Gentle Lady's control, annihilated the sect's primary base.

Later, during the crisis detailed in "Grant a Wish," having established a new sanctuary on a remote island, the Gentle Lady chose to intervene directly. She unleashed her Amorfi in a direct assault on the Ducklair Tower and, concurrently, attempted to sway Pikappa and Xadhoom to her cause, expertly preying upon their deepest regrets and frustrations. Yet, both heroes steadfastly resisted her insidious temptations, thwarting her menace once more.

In the dire events of "The Spider's Enemy," facing an impending planetary catastrophe, Uno and Dunia made the fateful decision to accept the Gentle Lady's proposal: to transport her to an alternate dimension. The consequence was dire: the world was plunged into a distorted semblance of a new Middle Ages, devoid of war and conflict, yet utterly devoid of freedom. Science was usurped by magic, the Ducklair Tower was reshaped into her tyrannical palace, and the entire planet was severed from the wider Universe.

Dunia Voyda and the sagacious monks of Dhasam-Bul managed to resist the Gentle Lady's pervasive mental dominion. However, Dunia's scientific acumen and the monks' occult wisdom proved equally impotent against her overwhelming powers. It was Pikappa, fortuitously absent on a mission in deep space and thus spared the initial enchantment, who, through his sheer practical ingenuity, discovered the means to liberate the world. By improvising a deliberately clumsy and ungainly ballet, he disrupted the delicate mystical rhythm of the Gentle Lady's own dances, shattering the illusory reality she had so meticulously constructed.

Refusing to acknowledge her defeat, the Gentle Lady ultimately retreated, transferring her very consciousness into her self-created imaginary dimension. There, she now perpetually deludes herself into believing she is a beloved and benevolent sovereign. Pikappa, facing an adversary rendered harmless and reduced to a catatonic state, ultimately entrusted her care to her remaining, devoted followers.
