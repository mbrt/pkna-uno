The Grifone was a lethal operative of the Organization, a formidable antagonist to PK. He was revealed to be the older, corrupted self of Trip, son of the Razziatore, warped by a terrible sequence of events.

## History

In a divergent timeline, Trip was utterly devastated by the death of his father, a tragedy for which he blamed both PK and the Tempopolizia. The Organization took him in, subjecting him to rigorous training, forging him into their most effective agent. Once he uncovered PK's secret identity, he unleashed a campaign of terror in the 20th century, operating as Commando Terminus. His heinous acts culminated in the destruction of the Ducklair Tower. He then provided Angus Fangus with falsified evidence, implicating PK in these atrocities and forcing the hero into hiding.

Later, the Organization assigned the Grifone a critical mission: to retrieve the child Trip (his younger self), whom Odin Eidolon had brought to 2015 to shield him from the Organization's influence, placing him under PK's care. Aided by the android Newton, the Grifone pursued his target with ruthless determination, even impersonating the Razziatore to achieve his goal. However, PK and the young Trip repeatedly evaded and resisted him, until the true Razziatore intervened decisively. With the collapse of that alternate reality, and Trip's conscious choice never to embrace a criminal path, the Grifone's very existence was erased from history.

## Character

The Grifone possessed all of the Razziatore's formidable abilities, including shape-shifting, but amplified them, adding even more capabilities like underwater navigation. He inherited his father's cunning, his love for risk, and his sarcastic wit, but utterly lacked his code of honor. The only discernible human trait within the Grifone appeared to be a flicker of tenderness towards his younger self, Trip.

## Physical Appearance

Physically, the Grifone was tall, powerful, and muscular, a stark contrast to the stockier build of his younger self. He wore formidable armor adorned with claw-shaped decorations and kept his red crest pulled back into a long ponytail.
