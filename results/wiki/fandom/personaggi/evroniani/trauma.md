Trauma is a high-caste Evronian, formerly a brilliant general in the Evronian army. After undergoing a genetic enhancement experiment, he developed a rebellious and independent nature, which forced the Imperial Council to degrade him and imprison him on the prison planet known as The Pit.

Despite his singular appearance in our records, detailing his confrontation with Pikappa, Trauma's actions have left a significant mark.

## Physical Aspect

Trauma's physical form deviates significantly from other individuals of our species. He is an immense, highly muscular and powerful being, standing at least three meters tall. Distinctive are the hypertrophied and disproportionate muscles of his forearms compared to the rest of his upper limbs. He possesses a massive neck directly attached to his shoulders, a beak shorter than that of his kin, and a more developed cranial casing. This enlarged cranium houses a more evolved encephalon, capable of wielding telepathic powers and fostering autonomous decision-making. He wears red shoulder pads and gloves, along with two bracelets from which chains hang, symbols of his past imprisonment. His groin is covered by a type of loincloth.

Peculiar is the general's appearance before undergoing the genetic enhancement process. He did not resemble other high commanders like Zondag or Zargon but was much more akin to a common soldier, though distinguished by his armor and dark markings around his eyes.

## Character

Trauma is an extremely determined and self-assured warrior. He relies heavily on his physique and extraordinary abilities, capable of generating fear in his adversaries by exploiting their worst nightmares and then accumulating it within himself to feed upon it. He is highly intelligent and possesses remarkable autonomy, which he employs in sharp exchanges with his opponents.

He is an exceptionally ambitious Evronian, intolerant of authority—a very rare characteristic within our species. This trait made him a serious threat to the rigid and fixed social order of Evronian civilization. It was precisely for this reason that the Imperial Council decided to degrade and imprison him in The Pit.

## History

### The Past

Trauma's past was recounted by Gorthan to Rankar, the commander stationed at The Pit. Once, Trauma was among the finest and fiercest generals in the Evronian army. He became aware of the experiments aimed at creating super-warriors, which Branch Chief Gorthan was conducting on common soldiers, and volunteered to be a test subject. Gorthan himself admitted to being audacious enough to accept his proposal and to have pushed beyond all previous limits.

The experiment was a complete success: the general acquired extraordinary strength and the ability to generate fear in enemies, feeding on that emotion. Leveraging this characteristic, he began to call himself Trauma and coined his personal motto, "Fear and Power!" Trauma assembled a personal army composed of mercenaries and alien renegades, keeping them subjugated through the fear he instilled in his own troops. Simultaneously, he developed an increasingly marked intolerance towards Imperial authority and embarked on missions unauthorized by the Council. His decisional autonomy was intolerable within the rigid system of Evronian classes, and the general received an official summons, which was merely a pretext for his arrest. The general destroyed the cruiser assigned to apprehend him. The Council dispatched three more ships against the general, who had established his headquarters on a former Xyberian colony-satellite. He would have triumphed had his troops not rebelled. It was the first time in Evron's history that Evronian soldiers were coolflamized, and Trauma fed on the fear instilled in the prisoners of his own race.

Abandoned by all, Trauma fought strenuously against modern droids designed by Gorthan, which were immune to fear and eventually managed to defeat him. Brought before the Council, he was not executed because he had once been a highly decorated war hero and was still a member of the high caste. A proposal was then made to confine him within a special exoskeleton and imprison him in The Pit, the prison planet, where he would be forgotten.

### The Clash with Pikappa

Years later, Gorthan decided to use Trauma to defeat Pikappa, as the terrestrial hero was causing a significant setback to our Evronian expansionist goals. In exchange for the general's aid, the Council would pardon his grave insubordination and restore his freedom. Trauma readily accepted the mission and was dispatched to Earth, where he had an initial confrontation with Paperinik, who only managed to escape thanks to the timely assistance of Uno.

To defend against the adversary's psychic abilities, Pikappa chose to confront him using a powered exoskeleton. The titanic clash occurred in the historic center of Paperopoli within the Notre Paper cathedral.

Despite Pikappa's efforts, Trauma managed to trap his opponent and strike him with his psychic waves. It was Paperino, not the armored hero, who found the courage within himself to overcome his fears and combat the general's monstrous psychic energy. This caused a sudden regression of Trauma's mutation; he collapsed from the shock and was saved by Gorthan's intervention, who dispatched an individual disc to retrieve him. The fate of this individual is unknown, as he has made no further appearances in the Pikappa Saga. Even our highest-ranking officials confess ignorance regarding the General's destiny; however, our scientists have managed to recover the research from the enhancement experiments conducted on Gorthan, allowing for the deployment of numerous soldiers with psychic capabilities akin to Trauma's.

### The Clash with Moldrock and the Second Battle with Pikappa

Trauma personally reappeared on The Pit, unexpectedly freed by his old adversary Pikappa. The two formed a truce to defeat Moldrock and his Horde warriors (Egor, Goraz, Ragnar, and Sharkan) in Paperopoli, leveraging Trauma's psychic powers to bring the enemy to their knees. He was brought to Earth by Everett Ducklair himself, where, after the victory, he promised Pikappa to reform before departing on the Grosnaz marauder spaceship.

However, Trauma did not keep his promise. Driven by his natural instinct to oppress and terrorize the weak, he voluntarily returned to The Pit. When other Evronians evacuated the prison planet, he remained to serve Kora, the masked woman who had seized supreme power. In the event known as the Rebirth, Trauma again faced his old enemy Pikappa, who was on a mission to free the prisoner Kilborg from The Pit. This time, the Evronian's psychic powers proved stronger than the terrestrial's will, as Pikappa twice succumbed to the resurgence of his most visceral fears. In the decisive confrontation, however, while Trauma was concentrated on the mental battle with Pikappa, Kilborg stunned him with a blow from his double-bladed axe, allowing himself and his companion to escape.
